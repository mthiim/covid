COVID-19 status in Denmark by regions

This page shows time series data of COVID-19 infections country-wide and in the different Danish regions. The data has been smoothed using 7-day moving averages.

Some graphs show the daily number of infected whereas others shows it normalized against population size (same measure as Ministry of Foreign Affairs is using).

The raw data is fetched directly from Statens Serum Institut (SSI) and will update when they publish a new file (usually happens on weekdays around 14).

New (December 5th): Now calculates the time-dependent reproduction number (Rt) shown as a dotted line (see scale on the right-hand side axis). This is new and experimental. It uses the R0 package from Wallinga & Teunis. I plan to add display of confidence intervals.

New (December 14th): Now shows the infection rate across different age groups (including relative rates), updated population municipality data.

New (December 29th): Note when comparing my Rt numbers to the official ones: It seems the official "contact numbers" in the news (today 1.2) are now calculated differently than before. Note in their latest graph, Rt is now never below 1 (even though they announced e.g. 0.9 on the 22th of December). It seems they have switched to using hospitalizations for the calculation instead, thereby introducing a long delay (1-2 weeks), maybe due to uncertainty in test numbers around the holidays. Also note, that the by-age graphs are temporarily disabled, since the corresponding data sheet from SSI is irregularly updated over Christmas (and unlike the other data, is not published as a time-series).

Note: Since the reported infections for a given date are often incomplete for 1-2 days, we ignore 2 days by default.
